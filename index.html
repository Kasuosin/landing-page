The Bridge Between Two Worlds: What is Management Information Systems (MIS)?

In today’s digital age, many people think that business and technology are two separate planets. On one side, you have the Business world—focused on people, profits, and strategy. On the other side, you have the Technology world—focused on coding, hardware, and data. Management Information Systems (MIS) is the bridge that connects these two worlds.

Not Just Coding, Not Just Management
A common mistake is thinking that MIS students are just "programmers who take business classes" or "managers who know a little bit of IT." In reality, MIS is about problem-solving. Imagine a large hospital that wants to use an AI system to track patient records. The doctors know what they need for patient care (Business), but they don’t know how to build the software. The software engineers know how to write the code (Technology), but they don’t understand the daily workflow of a hospital. An MIS professional steps in to translate these needs, ensuring the technology actually serves the business goals.
